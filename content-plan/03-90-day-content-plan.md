# 90-Day Content Plan

This is the high-level content plan for May 1-July 29, 2026. It keeps the first 30 days detailed in [02-30-day-content-plan-may-2026.md](./02-30-day-content-plan-may-2026.md), then gives broader direction for the following 60 days.

## 90-Day Strategic Goal

Use content to prove the central promise of Builder's Kit before asking people to buy:

> Business can be understood as a connected system, and a builder can learn the working version without drowning in books, courses, and scattered advice.

The 90 days should not feel like a course to the audience. It should feel like a serious daily business publication: useful today, useful tomorrow, and clearly coming from one coherent point of view.

Underneath, the 90 days should move the audience through three emotional states:

1. **Clarity** - "I finally understand how these concepts connect."
2. **Trust** - "This person explains business in a way that is useful and not hype."
3. **Intent** - "The kit is the complete version of what I have been learning for free."

## Phase Overview

| Phase | Dates | Content Job | CTA Posture | Main Output |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Phase 1 | May 1-May 30 | Build authority through standalone daily business clarity. | Free resources, newsletter, replies, concept library. | 30 daily videos, 4 syntheses, first topic clusters. |
| Phase 2 | May 31-June 29 | Deepen trust and lightly reveal the kit as the complete map. | Soft waitlist, lead magnets, behind-the-scenes previews. | 30 more videos, waitlist content, sample kit previews. |
| Phase 3 | June 30-July 29 | Convert attention into buyers or launch-ready demand. | Kit CTA, social proof, launch content, objections, buyer outcomes. | Launch month content engine or final pre-launch demand push. |

## Phase 1 - Days 1-30: Authority And Clarity

Dates: May 1-May 30, 2026.

Detailed daily plan: [02-30-day-content-plan-may-2026.md](./02-30-day-content-plan-may-2026.md).

### Content Themes

The themes should be mixed, not sequenced. Rotate through:

- business systems, types, and stages
- pricing, unit economics, and cash
- market, customer, competitor, and demand reality
- offer, positioning, and messaging
- GTM, sales, channels, and retention
- operations, delivery, people, and quality
- KPIs, finance, accounting, and decision rules
- a few famous-book hooks that map book lessons to business decisions

### What The Audience Should Learn

- A business is a system, not an idea.
- Different business types need different metrics and decisions.
- Market size is not proof.
- Pricing affects the entire business.
- Operations and finance are not late-stage topics.

### Main Formats

- Daily animated concept video.
- Short written post from each video.
- One weekly synthesis/newsletter.
- One or two blog/concept-page candidates per week.
- WhatsApp prompts that ask practical business questions.
- Three book-hook videos that preview the 30 Books Companion without turning the feed into book summaries.

### CTA Posture

No hard sell. Point people to free resources, newsletter signup, replies, and community discussion.

## Phase 2 - Days 31-60: Trust, Waitlist, And Deeper Application

Dates: May 31-June 29, 2026.

This phase should keep the daily educational cadence, but now the audience can start seeing the kit as the complete version of the free map.

If the product infrastructure is not ready, keep this as a waitlist-building and demand-validation phase. If the product is ready enough, introduce the founding-member waitlist gently.

### Days 31-60 Topic Pool: Trust And Deeper Application

Purpose: keep daily usefulness while showing more applied examples, sector translations, and behind-the-scenes pieces.

The feed should still mix topics. Do not publish this as a strict weekly sequence.

Marketing, sales, and distribution pool:

- Channel strategy by business type.
- Sales motion by price and complexity.
- Founder-led sales vs scalable sales.
- Messaging from customer language.
- Funnels and conversion assumptions.
- Retention, repeat purchase, and post-sale handoff.
- Book/framework option: The One Page Marketing Plan as a GTM structure lens.

Good sector examples:

- SaaS: activation and onboarding.
- Services: trust, referrals, proposals, and scoping.
- Manufacturing: distributors, procurement, and technical sales.
- Commerce: merchandising, paid acquisition, and repeat purchase.
- Marketplaces: supply and demand acquisition.

CTA posture: invite people to get the free pricing worksheet or join the newsletter. If the waitlist is ready, mention it softly at the end of 1-2 pieces.

Operations, people, and delivery pool:

- Operating model: how the promise gets delivered.
- Process design without bureaucracy.
- Quality assurance vs quality control.
- Inventory, fulfillment, and delivery reliability.
- Service scope, utilization, and founder dependency.
- Org structure, roles, decision rights, and communication.
- Leadership as a system of decisions, not personality.

Good sector examples:

- Services: utilization and delivery margin.
- Manufacturing: yield, defect rate, OTIF, and safety.
- Commerce: inventory turns, returns, fulfillment time.
- SaaS: support load and uptime.
- Hybrid: product layer vs service layer capacity.

CTA posture: invite people to reply with the part of their business that breaks first under growth.

Finance, accounting, cash, and KPIs pool:

- Revenue vs profit vs cash.
- Fixed costs, variable costs, OPEX, and CAPEX.
- Gross margin vs contribution margin.
- Unit economics by business type.
- Basic financial statements and what each one tells you.
- Budgeting, forecasting, and scenarios.
- KPI selection by stage and business type.

Good sector examples:

- SaaS: MRR, churn, CAC payback.
- Services: project margin, utilization, DSO.
- Manufacturing: gross margin per unit, capex, cash conversion cycle.
- Commerce: AOV, contribution margin after shipping and returns.
- Marketplace: GMV, take rate, liquidity, contribution per transaction.

CTA posture: free calculator, glossary, or concept page. If waitlist is open, position the kit as the place where all formulas and templates live together.

Strategy, risk, growth, and business plan pool:

- Strategy as resource allocation under constraint.
- Moats and defensibility by business type.
- Risk, compliance, and regulation.
- Expansion sequencing: segment, geography, channel, product line.
- Capital planning and fundraising readiness.
- Business plan as assembled logic, not a beautiful document.
- Weekly synthesis: from concept map to action plan.

Good book/framework options:

- Zero to One for defensibility.
- Blue Ocean Strategy for category and positioning.
- Lean Startup for evidence loops.

CTA posture: this is the strongest phase for waitlist content. Show how the kit pulls scattered concepts into one finishable system.

## Phase 3 - Days 61-90: Conversion, Proof, And The Complete Map

Dates: June 30-July 29, 2026.

This phase depends on product readiness.

If the kit is ready, use this as launch-month content. If the kit is not ready, use it as final pre-launch demand building while continuing to collect emails and waitlist signups.

### Days 61-90 Topic Pool: Conversion, Proof, And The Complete Map

Purpose: make the product feel like the natural complete version of the daily concepts, without turning every post into a pitch.

Use a mixed publishing rhythm across the following pools.

Launch or complete-system pool:

Purpose: show the full promise of Builder's Kit.

Content themes:

- The complete business map.
- Why scattered knowledge creates confusion.
- What the kit organizes: handbook, map, glossary, worksheets, exercises, quick-start paths.
- How a founder, agency owner, product seller, manufacturer, or operator would use the kit differently.
- Behind-the-scenes previews of the Connection Map, worksheets, glossary, and quick-start paths.
- Objection content: too basic, too advanced, too broad, too much to finish, not for my sector.

CTA posture: direct kit CTA if live. If not live, direct waitlist CTA.

Sector-specific application pool:

- SaaS/software business map.
- Service business map.
- Manufacturing business map.
- Commerce/product business map.
- Marketplace/platform business map.
- Hybrid business map.
- Weekly synthesis: the same principles, different operating logic.

CTA posture: point each sector to the relevant quick-start path, free concept cluster, or kit artifact preview.

Mistakes, diagnosis, and fixes pool:

- Mistaking market size for demand.
- Pricing before understanding cost-to-serve.
- Hiring before knowing bottlenecks.
- Tracking metrics without decision rules.
- Scaling channels before onboarding works.
- Mixing hybrid revenue streams into one confusing model.
- Writing a business plan before the thinking is done.

CTA posture: use practical diagnosis CTAs: download a checklist, join the list, get the kit, or reply with the mistake they are facing.

Famous frameworks mapped to the business system pool:

- Lean Startup: evidence loops and MVP discipline.
- Blue Ocean Strategy: positioning and market creation.
- Zero to One: defensibility and unique advantage.
- Never Split The Difference: sales conversations and negotiation.
- Building A StoryBrand: messaging clarity.
- One Page Marketing Plan: GTM structure.
- SaaS Playbook: software-specific metrics and why they are not universal.

CTA posture: position the 30 Books Companion as part of the kit, if launch-ready. Otherwise, use this week to test which book-derived ideas get the most demand.

### Final 2 Days - Days 89-90: Integration And Next Step

Purpose: end the 90-day arc with a clear sense of progress and a direct next step.

Daily topic pool:

- Day 89: how all 90 days connect into one business map.
- Day 90: what to do next based on your business type and stage.

CTA posture:

- If live: direct kit CTA with calm urgency if launch pricing or founding access is ending.
- If not live: waitlist CTA and clear date for the next release or preview.

## 90-Day Format Mix

Across the full 90 days, aim for this mix:

| Content Type | Share Of Output | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Educational concept explainers | 40 percent | Build trust and teach the map. |
| Connection chains | 20 percent | Prove that Builder's Kit is about connected thinking. |
| Sector applications | 15 percent | Keep the brand useful beyond SaaS/startups. |
| Book/framework/case lenses | 10 percent | Add memorability and cultural familiarity. |
| Personal/operator notes | 10 percent | Build trust in the editor behind the product. |
| Direct CTA | 5 percent in Phase 1, 10-15 percent in Phases 2-3 | Convert attention without turning the feed into ads. |

## 90-Day Content Assets To Build From The Daily Plan

By the end of 90 days, the content should have produced these reusable assets:

- 90 daily animated videos.
- 12 weekly Operator Notes or long-form syntheses.
- 12-20 blog or public concept-page drafts.
- A first public glossary cluster from repeated terms.
- A first Connection Map topic cluster.
- 3-4 lead magnet candidates.
- A list of audience questions and objections.
- A list of the highest-performing sectors and concept categories.

## Measurement Plan

Track weekly, not obsessively daily.

| Metric | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- |
| Videos shipped | Shows whether the engine is alive. |
| Saves and shares | Better signal of learning value than likes alone. |
| Comments and replies | Shows what people are trying to apply. |
| Email signups | Converts rented attention into owned audience. |
| WhatsApp replies | High-trust signal from existing communities. |
| Blog/concept page traffic | Shows which topics deserve deeper assets. |
| Waitlist signups | Shows commercial intent once Phase 2 starts. |
| Kit clicks or purchases | North-star conversion once Phase 3 starts. |

## Review Rhythm

Every Sunday, review:

- Which topic produced the strongest reaction?
- Which format worked best: explainer, chain, sector comparison, or framework?
- Which audience segment responded most: founders, operators, agency owners, product sellers, students, manufacturers, service owners?
- Which questions repeated?
- Which topic should become a blog post?
- Which topic should become a worksheet, calculator, glossary entry, or kit page?
- Are we still teaching across sectors, or drifting into one business type?

## Guardrails

- Do not make Phase 1 too sales-heavy.
- Do not let famous books become the main sequence.
- Do not overuse SaaS examples.
- Do not publish definitions without decision use.
- Do not explain concepts as if the audience is stupid.
- Do not chase trends unless they connect to the map.
- Do not expand to every platform with custom content. Create source content first, then adapt.
