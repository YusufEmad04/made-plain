# 30-Day Content Plan - May 2026

This is a detailed daily source-topic plan for May 1-30, 2026. It assumes one main 2D animated video per day, with each video repurposed into written posts, WhatsApp messages, blog sections, and weekly newsletter ideas.

This plan does not include final scripts or copy. It defines the daily topic, the reason for the topic, optional angles, and the best repurposing direction.

## Month Theme

**Standalone business clarity, connected behind the scenes.**

The audience should not feel like they are following a course. Every day should be useful even if someone discovers the account for the first time that day. Underneath, the topics rotate across the Builder's Kit map so the feed still feels intentional, professional, and complete over time.

## Month Goals

- Publish 30 daily animated concept videos.
- Make every post useful on its own.
- Avoid both extremes: random scattered tips and course-like sequencing.
- Establish the Builder's Kit voice: plain, useful, serious, warm, and anti-hype.
- Prove the core product idea through free content: business can be explained simply without making it shallow.
- Create reusable concept nodes for the future glossary, concept library, Connection Map, and kit.
- Touch all major business types: SaaS/software, services, manufacturing, commerce/products, marketplaces/platforms, and hybrids.

## Operating Cadence

Daily:

- 1 animated video.
- 1 short written post adapted from the video.
- 1 WhatsApp/community version when relevant.

Every 7-10 days:

- Check whether the feed has included strategy, marketing, operations, finance, people, metrics, and sector-specific examples.
- Write 1 long-form post or newsletter synthesis from the strongest topics.
- Convert repeated audience questions into future daily topics.
- Review what performed best and what questions people asked.

## Planning Logic

The plan below intentionally mixes topics. It uses a hidden rotation:

- core concept
- common mistake
- connection chain
- sector translation
- famous book/framework lens
- operator question
- practical metric or decision tool

Do not publish it as "Week 1: foundations" or "Week 2: market research." The public feed should feel like a sharp daily business publication, not a syllabus.

## End-Of-Month Review

At the end of May, review:

- Which topics got the most saves, shares, replies, and comments?
- Which business types produced the strongest audience reaction?
- Which questions repeated across platforms?
- Which concepts deserve blog posts or public concept pages?
- Which topics should become lead magnets, worksheets, or glossary entries?
- Did the content feel cross-sector, or did it drift too much toward SaaS?
