# Engines of Growth V6 Plan Review

Reviewed as a first-time viewer before scripting.

## What Works

- The plan now answers why viral audience matters: some businesses sell access to attention, demand, or network activity.
- Paid is planned as a moving money machine instead of a ledger, which should fix both overflow and comprehension.
- Trap is one example at a time, with hard wipes instead of crossfaded text.
- The diagnostic scene becomes a path, not stacked question cards.

## Risks Found

- Scene 06 was titled `06-viral-why`, but the existing composition router expects `06-viral-example`. The script should keep the existing id unless the router is renamed.
- Paid narration can exceed the two-minute budget if it overexplains both good and bad cases. It needs short sentences and visual math.
- Some planned labels could become tiny if they are placed inside narrow boxes. Use short labels only: `CAC`, `LTV`, `40 < 120`, `90 > 60`.
- Trap wipes must be non-overlapping. The outgoing panel should be gone before the incoming example text becomes readable.

## Required Fixes Before Script

- Keep `06-viral-example` as the scene id.
- Shorten paid narration while preserving CAC and LTV definitions.
- Add explicit overflow smoke checks for paid, trap, and diagnostic scenes.
- Prefer diagrams and numbers over full sentence labels inside boxes.
