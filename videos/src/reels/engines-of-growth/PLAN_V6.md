# Engines of Growth V6 Plan

Target: about 110 to 120 seconds. Educational tone. No ad language. Every abstract point gets a concrete visual example.

## 01-problem — Pick The Engine First

- Narration: Growth is not one thing. A subscription app, a social app, and a product store grow by different rules. First pick the engine.
- Visual: Three simple illustrated businesses enter a selector: subscription card, social network nodes, product box. The selector points to Sticky, Viral, Paid.
- Sync: subscription appears on "subscription app"; network appears on "social app"; product box appears on "product store"; selector locks on "engine".
- Critique check: avoids the old generic spinning-wheel metaphor and shows real business types immediately.

## 02-framework — Three Tests

- Narration: Sticky protects a base. Viral builds a crowd. Paid buys customers profitably. Each engine has one test.
- Visual: Three vertical test gauges draw in: base water line, crowd counter, profit scale.
- On-screen text: `STICKY: NEW > CHURN`, `VIRAL: K > 1`, `PAID: LTV > CAC`.
- Sync: each gauge draws when named; final tests align into a neat dashboard.
- Critique check: less text than the prior framework scene; each rule has a picture.

## 03-sticky — Bucket With Labels

- Narration: Sticky means retention. New customers pour in. Churn leaks out when people cancel. If twenty join and eight leave, the base rises. New must beat churn.
- Visual: Existing bucket animation, but with bigger labels and clearer arrows: `+20 new` enters, `-8 churn` leaks, water level rises.
- Sync: inlet starts on "pour in"; leak opens on "leaks out"; math settles on "base rises".
- Critique check: the tap and leak are now literal: new in, churn out.

## 04-sticky-example — Future Payments

- Narration: A subscription shows why. One happy customer pays again in April, May, and June. Cancel in March, and those future payments disappear.
- Visual: Calendar receipts animate across months. March cancellation stamp wipes out April, May, June receipts.
- Sync: receipts pop on month names; red cancel stamp lands on "Cancel in March".
- Critique check: explains why retention matters, not just what churn means.

## 05-viral — K Branches

- Narration: Viral means users bring users. K is the average number of new users each user creates. Above one, branches multiply. Below one, the chain fades.
- Visual: Existing branch animation, but with stronger path lines and a side meter moving from `K 1.3` to `K 0.6`.
- Sync: K badge appears on "K"; branches multiply on "Above one"; branches dim on "Below one".
- Critique check: keeps the good animation, makes the rule more legible.

## 06-viral-example — Audience Is The Product

- Narration: Why build an audience? Because some businesses sell access to the crowd. A marketplace needs buyers before sellers care. A media app needs attention before sponsors pay. The audience is the product.
- Visual: Animated two-sided marketplace. Buyers gather on the left, sellers on the right only move in after demand appears. Then a media screen fills with attention dots and a sponsor ticket appears.
- Sync: buyers gather on "buyers"; sellers enter on "sellers care"; sponsor ticket appears on "sponsors pay"; final crowd becomes the product on "audience is the product".
- Critique check: answers the user's WHY directly.

## 07-paid — Money Machine

- Narration: Paid is a money machine. CAC is the cost to win one customer. LTV is what that customer pays over time. If CAC is forty and LTV is one twenty, scale it. If CAC is ninety and LTV is sixty, each new customer deepens the loss.
- Visual: Animated acquisition machine. Coins enter ad funnel as CAC, one customer exits, repeat payments flow into LTV jar, then a balance scale compares CAC and LTV. Second pass flips to a loss case. Use short labels only: `CAC`, `LTV`, `40 < 120`, `90 > 60`.
- Sync: coins enter on "CAC"; payments fill jar on "LTV"; green scale on `40 < 120`; red scale on `90 > 60`.
- Critique check: removes ledger rows and clipped warning text; explains the actual business math.

## 08-trap — One Trap At A Time

- Narration: The wrong engine creates traps. Ads cannot save a leaky subscription. Buying isolated users cannot create a network. Going viral cannot fix a product that loses money.
- Visual: Three-panel carousel, but only one panel is visible at a time. Panel 1: ad coins pour into a leaking bucket. Panel 2: isolated paid users fail to connect. Panel 3: demand arrows multiply a negative margin tag.
- Sync: each example hard-wipes to the next after its sentence; no crossfade of text.
- Critique check: fixes the transition overlap and turns each trap into a tiny animation.

## 09-diagnostic — Decision Path

- Narration: Choose by asking one thing. Do customers repeat? Sticky. Do users make the network valuable? Viral. Do you buy customers one at a time? Paid.
- Visual: A decision path draws downward. Each question lights one branch and stamps the matching engine rule.
- Sync: branch draws on each question; answer stamp lands on the engine word.
- Critique check: easier than the old stacked question cards and uses the full vertical frame.

## 10-outro — Make The Equation True

- Narration: Pick the engine your business needs, then make its equation true.
- Visual: The three equations slide into one blank equation card. The selected engine locks in; final brand mark appears small.
- Sync: equations slide on "engine"; final equation locks on "true".
- Critique check: short ending, not promotional.

## Smoke-Test Targets

- Every scene: first 10%, midpoint, last 10%.
- Paid scene: CAC reveal, LTV reveal, green comparison, red comparison.
- Trap scene: before and after every carousel wipe.
- Viral why scene: buyer/seller transition and sponsor/attention transition.
- Diagnostic: every branch reveal.
- Overflow checks: paid comparison, trap wipe, diagnostic branch text.
