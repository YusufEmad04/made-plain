# Engines of Growth V6 Critique

## Viewer Problems To Fix

- The current paid scene overflows horizontally. The red warning pill clips off-screen, and the ledger rows become too dense for a reel viewport.
- The trap scene overlaps old and new examples during transitions. A viewer sees multiple labels at once, so the point becomes unreadable.
- Several scenes rely on text in boxes instead of animation. Sticky and viral have visible motion, but paid, traps, and diagnostic feel like slides.
- Some text is too small for mobile viewing, especially progress labels, row descriptions, and dense mono labels.
- The viral audience example says audience matters but does not fully answer why. It needs to show that some businesses sell access to attention, demand, or network activity.
- Spacing is inconsistent. Some frames have cramped top/middle text, while other frames leave the lower half underused.
- The visuals do not always sync tightly to the narration. The viewer should see the diagram change at the same moment the voice names the idea.

## First-Time Viewer Standard

A first-time viewer should be able to answer four questions without pausing:

1. What are the three engines?
2. What does each engine need to be true?
3. Why does that engine matter for that kind of business?
4. What mistake happens when the wrong engine is used?

## V6 Direction

- Keep the tone plain and educational, not promotional.
- Shorten narration so the finished reel lands close to two minutes.
- Replace static text blocks with moving diagrams: buckets, branching users, marketplace/audience flow, paid acquisition machine, trap carousel, and diagnostic decision path.
- Use one visible example at a time in the trap scene. No cross-faded example text.
- Use large text only for labels that must be read instantly. Put details into diagrams, numbers, and motion.
- Smoke-test start, middle, end, and transition frames for every scene, with extra checks on paid and trap scenes.
